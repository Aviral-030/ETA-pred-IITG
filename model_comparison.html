What the Numbers Mean for Operations
📈 15%-Accuracy: +28.7% Relative Gain
The GNN correctly predicts 39.95% of trips within ±15% of actual, vs 31.05% for the baseline. Roughly 1 in 11 previously mispredicted trips is now correctly estimated — a direct reduction in SLA breaches.
📉 MAE: −8.3% Lower Prediction Error
Mean absolute error drops 1.0639 → 0.9758 on the delay ratio. The GNN's average prediction is closer to actual delivery time by ~0.09 units per trip — compounding across thousands of daily shipments into real planning gains.
📊 R²: Nearly 2× Variance Explained
The GNN captures 23.2% of delay variance vs 12.0% for the baseline. Both remain modest due to high logistical noise, but the GNN extracts meaningfully more signal by incorporating the full network graph structure.
🧠 Why: Hub Reputation Embeddings
The baseline treats every trip in isolation using 7 features. The GNN learns a 16-dim embedding per hub across 3 message-passing rounds — so a hub's chronic delay history is baked into every prediction it participates in.
